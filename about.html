About

I am a senior physics major at Reed College, in Portland, OR. My interests include quantum computing, solid-state theory, and field theory. I’m currently writing a thesis on the simulation of open quantum systems, advised by Darrell Schroeter.

I am also a training supervisor and senior reactor operator at the Reed Research Reactor, responsible for implementing and managing a student-led training program to prepare trainees for Nuclear Regulatory Commision licensing exams, and furthering scientific research at the reactor facility.

Outside of physics, I am very interested in economics—especially the economics of networks and the role of information in creating efficient markets. After graduating from Reed, I hope to pursue a Ph.D. in economics, focusing on these topics.

In my free time, I enjoy long-distance train travel, kayaking, and cooking. I’m hoping to add more information on these things to the site at some point—stay posted!
